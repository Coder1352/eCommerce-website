Types of drones.
Most drones fall into one of two categories: camera drones and toy drones. Racing drones represent a unique version of camera drones and offer competitive but friendly interaction with other drone owners. All drones have features and benefits that vary based on the type.

Camera drones.
Also known as professional or enthusiast drones, these high-end models are typically camera drones. They have integrated cameras capable of high-definition (HD) and 3D images. The latest models have extraordinary capabilities:

-Altitudes of a mile or more
-A flying range of 5 miles or more
-Top speeds of 70 mph
-Battery life exceeding half an hour

Many of these are fine for beginners. Some require an experienced hand on the controls, but the internal stabilizing mechanisms help the drone fly smoothly, regardless of the pilot's ability.

Racing drones.
This group is a subset of professional drones. These enhanced models offer increased speed and maneuverability. They require HD cameras to provide the operator with a clear view of the racing course.

Toy drones.
Toy drones are simple models designed for basic flight. They are uncomplicated, safe toys that provide entertainment and outdoor fun for all ages. Some of the high-end toy drones are hard to distinguish from the lower-end camera drones. True enthusiasts might admit that their souped-up professional drones are really awesome toys.

Water drones.
Water drones are not for flying but are instead used for exploring underwater. They offer the same wireless control and precision image-capturing qualities as the airborne drones in a submersible form. Water and fishing enthusiasts enjoy exploring beneath the surface of saltwater and freshwater environments through the lenses of these drones.
